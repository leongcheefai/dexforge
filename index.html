About PaperDex

PaperDex is a free, browser-based tool for generating printable Pokémon binder placeholder PDFs. Collectors use it to fill empty slots in their Pokémon card binders with custom-designed placeholders while they search for the real cards. No account or installation is required — everything runs directly in your browser and your data stays on your device. PaperDex was built by a collector for collectors, with a focus on speed, simplicity, and high-quality print output that looks great on any home printer.

How to Build Your Page Preview

Select Pokémon by generation preset, name search, or Pokédex number range. Choose from seven sprite styles — including Gen 1–9 pixel art and official artwork. Customize the card color, color background gradient, font family, and whether to show the Pokédex number on each card. The live 3×3 page preview updates in real time as you make changes. When you are ready, export a print-ready PDF or use your browser's built-in print dialog to send it straight to your printer.

Pixel Art Sprite Styles & Features

  • All 1,000+ Pokémon supported via PokéAPI

Who Is It For?

PaperDex is built for Pokémon TCG collectors who want neat, consistent placeholders in their card binders. Whether you are building a living Pokédex binder, organizing sets by generation, or simply keeping track of missing cards, PaperDex lets you print professional-looking placeholder cards in minutes. It is equally useful for teachers and parents who want to create printable Pokémon activity sheets for kids.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PaperDex free to use?
Yes. PaperDex is completely free with no hidden costs, no account required, and no watermarks on exported PDFs.
Which Pokémon are supported?
All Pokémon from Generation 1 through Generation 9 are supported, covering all 1,000+ species from Bulbasaur to Pecharunt.
Can I customize the card design?
Yes. You can change the border color, background gradient, font, sprite style, and toggle the Pokédex number on or off for each batch.
Does it work offline?
Once sprites and Pokémon names are loaded for the first time, they are cached in your browser so subsequent sessions work without a network connection.
What print size do the placeholders use?
Cards are sized to match standard Pokémon card dimensions (63 × 88 mm). Each exported PDF page fits a 3×3 grid of nine placeholder cards, ready to slot directly into binder pages.
Can I save my card design as a preset?
Yes. Your customization settings — including border color, color background, font, and sprite style — are saved automatically in your browser so they are ready next time you visit.
